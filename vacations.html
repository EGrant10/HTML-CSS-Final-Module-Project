Traveling the World

One of the most beneficial things that have come from going out on my own is my eyes opening to how big the world is. I think in college you have the idea that it would be cool to travel but you don't think much about why you want to do it. Even more so most people never even actually take that first step. The past 4 years of traveling I've done since college has not only allowed me to meet incredible people, but has showed me just how beautiful God's world is and how much I should appreciate it by exploring it. There is so much difference in culture in different places and I love experiencing it first hand. Traveling has become a passion of mine that has even made me want to live abroad at some point. Below I'm going to recap some of my best trips so far.

Japan

In 2023, I spontaneously decided to take a trip for my 25th birthday to Japan. I decided I wanted to make sure I got the most out this experience so I decided to go with a travel company I had heard about in college. Basically you sign up and when you arrive to your destination you meet up with other solo travelers along with a tour guide. It was incredible to experience Japan in this way. Our tour guide was native to Japan so he knew all the best spots to take us to. We went to Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka over the course of 10 days.

While in Japan I fell in love with the culture there. As a naturally more introverted person myself, I resonated with the calmness and respect everyone showed each other. Their culture is so rich in history in uniqueness that it made every thing I did there so intersting. From visiting different temples, to seeing Mt. Fuji, to even just going to the Mcdonalds there. I think people often have a hesitency to go to countries like Japan because it is so homogenous, and I obviously stuck out there but everyone was so friendly with me. I not only felt the respect in my interactions but watching how Japanese people interacted with each other. Now I'm not naive enough to beleive that there are never any disagreements or crime that happens there, but the tone of the society was very different from here in America. I already have plans to go back this coming year and would even like to live there for a short stint some day.

Greece

In 2024, I used the same company I had used for my Japan trip and went to Greece. This was a little different though, I invited one of my best friends from highschool to go with me this time and we had a great time. We went to Athens, Mykonos, Paros, and Santorini. It was pretty cool seeing such an ancient country that has had such a huge impact on architecture, art, and philosophy. The real highlight of this trip though was the food. I had the best food of my life in Greece. From fresh seafood right by the water, lamb, souvlaki, and of course the taziki and pita. It was all incredible and I would go back just for the food alone.

Mexico

This year I went to Cozumel, Mexico with my mom on a cruise. This was your typicl mother son trip and honestly it was very chill. I've been away from home for 4 years now so our dynamic is still mother son but we're both adults now so it's a little different. I love getting to introduce her to things that she typically wouldn't do, like I signed us up for a cave hike in the cenotes. I think we hiked something like 3 miles completely in their underground cave system. At some points we were just walking in knee deep water and other points we were swimming where our feet couldn't touch the ground. The whole trip was a lot of fun and I'm grateful for the time with my mom.
