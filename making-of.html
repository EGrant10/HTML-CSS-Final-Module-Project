Where it Started

My life began where a lot of greats have come from (Lebron), northeast Ohio. I was born in the city of Youngstown as an only child to a 19 year old college student. My experiences growing up as an only child with not a lot of other kids around shaped me into an incredibly introspective person. Becoming comfortable with my own company has allowed me to develop a strong sense of self and a vivid imagination. I spent a lot of time reading books and playing video games which sparked my love for storytelling and creativity.

Where it Continued

When I was 11, my mom and I moved from all of our family to Greensboro, NC. At the time, this was a very hard transition for me. It was changing everything I had known up to that point. Having all my grandparents around, aunts, uncle, cousins, and friends that I had known since kindergarden were all taken away. However, I look at this change in my life as one of the greatest choices my mom ever made for me. As much as I am appeeciative of my childhood it wasn't the best place to grow up in. There was a lot of crime and not a ton of opportunity, and looking back as an adult there is this almost feeling of complacency to never leave. Moving to Greensboro was a first step in me understanding how there may be more out there outside of my bubble. While in North Carolina I gained lifelong friendships and had incredible experiences that shaped the man I am today. I was in Greensboro until I graduated college in 2021.

I went to college in 2017 at North Carolina A&T State University where I got my degree in Industrial and Systems Engineering. My time at A&T was definitely a very unique experience for me. For most of the schooling in my life up to that point I was one of, if not the only, african american kid in my classes. There were a few times growing up where it stuck out to me because of things said but I grew up with a lot of friends from different backgounds so it was never something that burdened me. Going to the largest HBCU in the country was a huge difference, and not for the reason people would think. It wasn't because we all were the same because we weren't. We may have been a similar ethnicity, but I met people from Atlanta, New York City, Chicago, people from all over who had completely different experiences than me and different thoughts on things. Maybe ironically, going to an HBCU made me more aware that people are people. Who your are as a person is unequivocally more important and valuable than what you look like.

Where it's Going

Some time has passed since I graduated college, I've been in Houston, TX for the past 3 years and this has been the most growth I've experienced in my life. I've met incredible people and have had my horizons broadened in ways I couldn't have imagined. Moving to a new city where I had never been and had no roots or family around has been the soil where I have grown mentally, physically, and most importantly spiritually. Above all else my relationship with my Lord and Savior Jesus Christ has grown so much in the past 4 years. He has been my peace and guide for growing into the man I am today along with who I am becoming.
